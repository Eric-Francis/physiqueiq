# PHYSIQUEIQ Research Hierarchy

## Purpose

This document defines the order of scientific evidence that PHYSIQUEIQ will trust when generating coaching recommendations.

Every recommendation generated by the platform must be traceable to evidence.

If conflicting evidence exists, higher quality evidence always takes priority.

---

# Level 1 — Highest Confidence

Systematic Reviews

Meta-Analyses

Umbrella Reviews

Clinical Practice Guidelines

Position Stands from recognized scientific organizations

Examples:

- ACSM
- NSCA
- ISSN
- IOC
- BJSM

---

# Level 2

Randomized Controlled Trials

Controlled Clinical Trials

Large Prospective Studies

---

# Level 3

Cohort Studies

Case-Control Studies

Longitudinal Studies

---

# Level 4

Cross-Sectional Studies

Observational Studies

Population Studies

---

# Level 5

Expert Consensus

Professional Guidelines

Experienced Coaches

---

# Level 6

Mechanistic Research

Laboratory Studies

Biomechanical Analysis

EMG Studies

---

# Level 7

Case Reports

Case Series

---

# Level 8

Expert Opinion

Conference Talks

Educational Material

---

# Never Used Alone

Social Media

Influencers

Marketing Material

Blogs without references

Anonymous Sources

AI-generated claims without citations

---

# Research Requirements

Every knowledge object should include:

Evidence Level

Citation

Publication Year

Authors

Journal

DOI

Population Studied

Limitations

Confidence Score

Last Review Date

---

# Research Review Policy

Scientific knowledge evolves.

Every knowledge object should be reviewed periodically to ensure recommendations remain aligned with the latest evidence.

Older evidence may remain valid but should always be evaluated against newer systematic reviews and high-quality research.
